The Flywheel: How the Three Lines Reinforce Each Other

Consultancy is the front door. A foreign pharma company engages Amana to plan its Malaysia entry (Line 3). During registration — which takes 12–24 months — the same client needs SAM/NPP supply for early patients and may run a local clinical trial requiring IMP import (Line 1). At launch, the client needs a Patient Support Program for its high-cost specialty drug (Line 2), and Amana becomes its appointed distributor (existing core business). One client, four revenue streams, multi-year relationship.

Strategic Logic

  • Monetises regulatory expertise Amana already pays for
  • Deepens hospital relationships built by the NPP institution campaign
  • Diversifies revenue away from pure distribution margin
  • Creates switching costs — service clients rarely change distributors
  • Builds the credibility base for the Phase 1 B2B ePharma platform

What We Are NOT Proposing

  • No retail or consumer pharmacy channel
  • No owned logistics infrastructure (continue outsourcing)
  • No manufacturing or licensing-in of products
  • No acquisition — all three lines are organic builds
  • No headcount beyond 5–7 specialist hires across 3 years

Recommendation

Approve all three lines as a single program. Launch consultancy immediately (lowest cost, fastest revenue, generates leads), file CTIL capability application in parallel (longest regulatory lead time), and begin PSP capability build in Q3. Total program investment under RM 800K, fundable from operating cash flow.

Market Context

Malaysia recorded 267 sponsored clinical research studies in 2024, with cumulative sponsored research growing at ~12% CAGR over five years. Over 300 healthcare facilities now have clinical research experience, and AstraZeneca has been the top sponsor three years running (2023–2025). Every interventional drug trial requires an unregistered investigational product to be imported under a Clinical Trial Import Licence (CTIL) issued by NPRA under CDCR 1984 — governed by the Malaysian CTIL/CTX Guideline (Edition 8.1, effective March 2025). This is the same regulatory family as Amana's Regulation 15(6) SAM work: importing unregistered medicines, with documentation, pharmacovigilance, and accountability.

The Gap Amana Fills

  • Sponsors and CROs need a local CTIL holder. Foreign sponsors cannot hold the licence themselves — a Malaysian entity must apply, hold, and manage it. Many use ad-hoc arrangements with CRO affiliates or generalist distributors.
  • IMP logistics is specialist work: temperature-controlled receipt, GMP-compliant storage, site-by-site distribution, returns and destruction with full chain-of-custody — the long tail that big distributors deprioritise.
  • Comparator drug sourcing (registered drugs used as trial comparators) is a recurring sponsor pain point Amana's principal network can solve.
🎯

Target Customers

Primary: Regional CROs running Malaysian sites (Novotech, IQVIA, Parexel local operations) and mid-size biotech sponsors without a Malaysian entity. Secondary: investigator-initiated trials at UMMC, UKMMC, IKN — sites already on Amana's NPP target list. Entry: CRM (Clinical Research Malaysia) partner ecosystem and the IPESS section relationships at NPRA.

Patient Support Program (PSP) Management

Run affordability, access, and adherence programs for MNC specialty drugs in Malaysia

Market Context

Asia-Pacific pharmaceutical services outsourcing was a USD 19.2 billion market in 2023, projected to reach USD 35.2 billion by 2033. In Southeast Asia, the dominant PSP type is the affordability program — "Buy X Get Y Free" patient access schemes for expensive specialty drugs (mAbs, targeted oncology agents, biologics) — alongside adherence and nurse-educator programs. Every MNC launching a high-cost drug in Malaysia needs one. Today they buy this service from Zuellig's PatientCare+ or DKSH's Patient Solutions — the same two generalists Amana's competitive landscape report identified as strong-but-distracted. Their PSP units serve their biggest distribution clients first; smaller MNCs and single-product launches get standardised, low-touch programs.

Market Access & Regulatory Consultancy

Paid advisory for foreign pharma entering Malaysia — and the lead-generation engine for Lines 1 & 2

Market Context & The Gap

Foreign manufacturers cannot deal with NPRA directly — they need a locally incorporated Product Registration Holder (PRH) and practical navigation of QUEST 3+, evaluation pathway selection (full vs. abbreviated review for FDA/EMA/PMDA-approved drugs), MOH formulary listing, and government tender mechanics. Existing options are global regulatory consultancies (Freyr, Artixio, PECAN — strong on paperwork, weak on hospital reality) or the big distributors (who consult only to capture distribution). Amana's edge: it actually operates in Malaysian hospitals daily. Advice on SAM pathways, formulary committees, pharmacy procurement behaviour, and realistic pricing comes from practice, not desk research.
