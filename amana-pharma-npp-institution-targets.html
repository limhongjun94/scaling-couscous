5 Scoring Dimensions

  • Oncology Patient Volume — higher volume = more SAM applications filed per year
  • Therapeutic Complexity — institutions treating rare cancers, haematological malignancies, and orphan diseases have the highest NPP need
  • Access to Novel Agents — institutions running clinical trials or with specialist oncologists are early adopters of unregistered drugs
  • Institutional Formulary Gaps — public hospitals with restricted formularies drive the most SAM filings for off-formulary or unregistered drugs
  • Decision-Maker Access — ease of reaching the pharmacy director, head of oncology, or medical affairs team

⚠️

Public vs. Private SAM Pathway — Know the Difference

Public (MOH) hospitals: SAM applications go through the Hospital Pharmacy Director → State Pharmaceutical Officer → Director of Pharmaceutical Services (DPS), MOH. Amana's role is to supply the approved drug. Relationship must be built with both the clinician filing the application and the pharmacy team executing the order.
Private hospitals: More flexible — the treating specialist drives the SAM application directly. Relationship with the specialist/oncologist is the primary entry point. Private pharmacy directors also influence preferred supplier selection.

Key Insight: Concentrate First, Then Expand

Winning 3–4 Tier 1 accounts (IKN, HKL, Hospital Ampang, Beacon) gives Amana credibility to rapidly open all other doors. Oncologists and pharmacy directors share supplier intelligence. A reference from a national referral centre is the most powerful sales tool in this market.

Section 5 — Outreach Strategy & Talking Points AMANA PHARMA
How Amana Should Approach Each Institution
Proven BD tactics tailored to the NPP/SAM procurement context in Malaysian hospitals

Who to Meet First

  • Public hospitals: Hospital Director of Pharmacy (Pengarah Farmasi) — they control SAM procurement approvals and supplier selection. Followed by the Head of Oncology / Haematology who writes the prescriptions.
  • Private hospitals: Lead Medical Oncologist or Haemato-Oncologist first — they drive drug selection. Then Pharmacy Director for supplier onboarding.
  • Teaching hospitals: Head of Department + Head of Pharmaceutical Services — dual entry is required as academic physicians also influence formulary additions.

Opening Conversation Points

  • "We specialise in Special Approval Medicines under Regulation 15(6) — we manage the entire application process so your team doesn't have to."
  • "We have relationships with principals across oncology, rare disease, and specialty therapeutics — including drugs not yet registered in Malaysia."
  • "Our turnaround on SAM documentation and supply is typically 2–4 weeks faster than generalist distributors."
  • "We cover all 4 therapeutic areas: oncology, rare disease, chronic NCDs, and general specialty — one relationship for all your unregistered medicine needs."

Documents to Bring

  • Amana Pharma company profile with SAM/NPP track record (include case examples anonymised)
  • List of principals / drug portfolio accessible via Amana's SAM channels
  • A one-page SAM process flowchart showing Amana's role at each step
  • Cold chain and GDP compliance certificates
  • Reference letters from existing hospital pharmacy clients
  • NPRA import license and MOH Type A special conditions distributor appointment letter

Differentiator Hooks by Institution Type

  • National referral centres (IKN, HKL, Hospital Ampang): Emphasise breadth — Amana can supply across all therapeutic areas, not just one category.
  • East Malaysia (HUS, KPJ Sabah): Emphasise reliability of East Malaysia supply and cold chain commitment — this is their biggest pain point.
  • Rare disease hospitals (HRPB, HSNZ, UKMMC): Emphasise Amana's orphan drug access and familiarity with NPRA's Malaysian Orphan Medicines Guideline 2020.
  • New oncology centres (Thomson/OncoCare, Island/Icon): Emphasise Amana as a founding preferred supplier before competitors establish relationships.
