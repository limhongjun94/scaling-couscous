HeyDoc's Core Strategy: "Intel Inside"

HeyDoc does not compete at the consumer layer — it embeds its infrastructure into existing healthcare providers (hospitals, clinics, insurers, employers) as a B2B2C layer. Its three products together form a complete digital health operating system from consultation → drug pricing → capitated outpatient management. Amana's counter-strategy should focus on the drug supply chain and NPP specialty layers that HeyDoc does not serve.

Regulatory Framework (Key Facts)

  • SAM = Malaysia's NPP equivalent — officially "Special Approval Medicines" under Reg. 15(6) CDCR 1984
  • Approving authority: Director General of Health (DG Health) / Director of Pharmaceutical Services (DPS)
  • NPRA oversees licensing of importers; maintains QUEST System list
  • MOH Pharmaceutical Services manages public hospital SAM workflow
  • Companies must hold an Import Licence (Type A Special Conditions) to supply unregistered medicines
  • CTIL (Clinical Trial Import Licence) — separate framework for investigational products in trials
  • Relevant policy documents: Malaysian Orphan Medicines Guideline 2020; National Rare Disease Policy (NRDP) 2025

Designated Rare Disease Centres (MOH)

  • Hospital Kuala Lumpur (HKL) — Genetics & rare disease national referral
  • Hospital Raja Permaisuri Bainun (HRPB), Ipoh — Perak state + northern Malaysia
  • Hospital Sultanah Nur Zahirah (HSNZ), Terengganu — East Coast referral
  • Three university hospitals co-manage rare disease: UMMC, UKMMC, HUSM
  • National Rare Disease Committee (NRDC) established 2019, chaired by Deputy DG Health

EAP Awareness Gap — Amana's Opening

A 2024 study of Malaysian oncologists found 80% filed 1–10 EAP applications in the past 12 months, but 73% received NO institutional training on EAPs. Amana can run CME-accredited EAP education programs — becoming the trusted educator = trusted distributor.
