What is NPP / Special Access Medicine?

A Named Patient Programme (NPP) — known in Malaysia as a Special Access Medicine (SAM) — is the formal pathway that allows hospitals to obtain pharmaceutical products that are not yet registered with Malaysia's NPRA (National Pharmaceutical Regulatory Agency). These are life-saving medicines approved in the US, EU, or Japan, but not yet commercially available in Malaysia.

In Malaysia, this access is governed by Regulation 15(6) of the Control of Drugs and Cosmetics Regulations (CDCR) 1984, which permits the importation of unregistered products for treatment of life-threatening illnesses — subject to pre-approval from the Director of Pharmaceutical Services.

Amana Pharma is a licensed, specialist importer operating under this framework. Our regulatory expertise, international supplier relationships, and documentation capability make us the trusted link between hospital pharmacists and the medicines their patients urgently need.

Why This Market Exists and Will Keep Growing

New cancer drugs approved in the US or EU typically take 3 to 7 years to receive Malaysian registration. During this window, the only legal way for Malaysian patients to access them is through a Special Access / NPP application fulfilled by a licensed specialist importer.

With the global oncology pipeline producing more new molecular entities than any other therapeutic area, and Malaysia's National Rare Disease Policy launched in 2025 expanding formal access to orphan medicines — this market grows larger every year.

Amana Pharma operates at the heart of this gap.

The Regulatory Pathway

Importing unregistered medicines in Malaysia is tightly regulated. Understanding and navigating this pathway requires specialist expertise — which is precisely what Amana Pharma provides.

How a Special Access Application Works

Oncologist Identifies Unregistered Medicine Need
A specialist determines that the best treatment for their patient is not available in Malaysia as a registered product.
Hospital Pharmacist Submits SAM Application
Under Regulation 15(6) CDCR 1984, the hospital pharmacy department applies to the Director of Pharmaceutical Services for pre-approval to import the unregistered product.
Director of Pharmaceutical Services Grants Approval
The application is assessed and, if approved, a permit is granted for importation of the specific product for the specific patient or institution.
Amana Pharma Sources and Imports the Medicine
As a licensed importer under Regulation 12(1)(d) CDCR 1984, Amana Pharma uses its international supplier network to source the product, manages all documentation (CPP, CoA, stability data), handles customs, and delivers to the hospital.
Medicine Reaches the Patient
The hospital pharmacy dispenses the medicine to the patient under the approved permit.

Malaysia launched its first National Rare Disease Policy (NRDP) in August 2025 — a landmark development that is expanding the formal framework for accessing orphan and special medicines. Key developments include:

  • National Rare Disease Committee (NRDC) established with subcommittees
  • National Rare Disease List officially published
  • Malaysian Orphan Medicines Guideline 2020 in active use
  • Patient Access Scheme (PASc) — MOH-pharma company collaborations for high-cost medicines
  • Health Insurance Department exploring inclusion of orphan drugs in national health insurance (in progress)
  • Orphan medicine status milestone: Zolgensma (SMA gene therapy) granted Orphan Medicine status in Malaysia in 2024
What the NRDP 2025 Means for Amana Pharma

As Malaysia formalises its rare disease framework and works toward national health insurance inclusion of orphan medicines, the legitimate procurement channel for special access medicines grows larger.
