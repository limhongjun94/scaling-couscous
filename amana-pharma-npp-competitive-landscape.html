Regulatory Foundation: What is NPP / SAM in Malaysia?

In Malaysia, the equivalent of a "Named Patient Programme" operates under Regulation 15(6) of the Control of Drugs and Cosmetics Regulations (CDCR) 1984. These are called Special Approval Medicines (SAMs) — medicines used with approval from the Director General of Health when standard registered therapeutic options are insufficient or ineffective for a specific patient.

SAMs are primarily accessed through MOH hospitals and private healthcare institutions for life-threatening conditions such as cancer, rare diseases, and refractory chronic conditions. The National Pharmaceutical Regulatory Agency (NPRA) maintains a licensed importer registry, and the MOH Pharmaceutical Services Division maintains a list of "Appointed Distributors Under Special Conditions of Type A License."

📋

Key Regulatory Players

NPRA — Issues licenses, maintains the QUEST System for licensed importers & approved SAM listings.
MOH Pharmaceutical Services — Manages the SAM approval process for public hospitals; approves distributor appointments.
Director General of Health — Final authority for SAM approvals under Regulation 15(6).

The Hidden Weakness of Large Distributors

Zuellig and DKSH are generalists at heart — NPP/SAM is one capability among dozens. Their specialty divisions serve large MNC clients (Novartis, Eli Lilly, Chiesi) with high-volume product launches. Individual patient SAM applications, small-volume specialty orders, niche oncology protocols, and underfunded hospital pharmacy requests often fall through the cracks. Amana's focus and agility become an advantage in the long tail of unregistered medicine access.
